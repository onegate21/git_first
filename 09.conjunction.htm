09.  접속사 (단어 혹은 문장을 이어 주는 접착제 ) 　 　 　 　 　 　
- 구조 : ①  접속사 S+V, S+V.   ②  S+V 접속사 S+V.
1) 등위 접속사
    - and / or / but / so
And I am walking and talking with a man. (그리고)
Or You can join us or wait for Ara. (혹은, 또는)
Is he your boyfriend or not?
Or【 명령문 】 Pay it back or else! (아니면 확!!)
Wash the dishes, or else!
But  She is cute but cranky. (그러나)
But【 전치사 】 I don't want anything but you. (~를 제외하고)
I like nothing but this.
But【 부사 】 He is but a child. (겨우, 단지)
So i didn't like him so I asked him to leave. (그래서)
So【 목적 】 I don't pay attention to rumors so I can focus (~를 위해서)
on my work better.
2) 시간 접속사
   - while / when / as / since / after / before / until / once / by the time
While While I was studying, you were having fun! (~동안)
I enjoy seafood, while Arty doesn't. (~반면에)
When When I am alone, I get frightened. (~ 때)
As As I was talking to her, I realized she was done (~할 때)
with me.
Since We've been together since she came to Korea. (~이후로)
It's been 3 years since we graduated from high school.
Since【 이유 】 Since I didn't speak English well, it was hard to (~때문에)
make friends.
He couldn't join us, since he was ill. (~왜냐하면)
After After he realized his dream, he become famous. (~이후에)
Before I wil get him a car before he gets a job. (~이전에)
Before I return home, you should finish your homework.
Until Until I get back to you, you shouldn't do anything silly. (~때 까지)
Until the chocolate melts, don't deep the marshmallow in it.
Once Once you move to Korea, I will find a job. (한번 ~하면)
Once you apply yourself, you will be successful.
By the time By the time you finish this course, you will speak (~쯤에)
English fluently.
By the time it is over, they will be all dead.
3) 장소 접속사
Where Where there is water, there's life. (~곳에)
4) 이유 접속사
   - because / as / since / for
Because Because she was so cute, I tolerated her. (~때문에)
Because he cried like a girl, I wasn't attracted to him.
As He might need some financial assistance as he is poor.
For S+V(결과) for S+V(원인)
I need to listen to her carefully for she can give me 
a job in the future.
The office was closed, for it was a national holiday.
Leave me alone, for I am tired.
5) 조건 접속사
   - if / in case / unless
If If someone needs me, I will be in my office. (만약~라면)
In case My mom put a chocolate bar in my bag in case (혹시모를 상황에 대비해)
I get hungry.
Take an umbrella in case it rains.
Unless You'll get fat unless you stop eating ice cream. (~하지 않으면)
Unless he was very ill, he would be at work. (~가 아니라면)
6) 양보 접속사 (두 절이 서로 상반되는 내용)
   - though / although / even though / even if / if / whether
   - though S+V(+), S+V(-) / though S+V(-), S+V(+)
Though Thoug Korea is a very small country, (비록 ~지만)
its culture is rich and diverse.
Thoug he changed himself a lot, nothing got better.
Although Although she was rich, she lived simply. (~임에도 불구하고)
Although he was tired, he couldn't fall asleep.
Even though #사실이 있고, 그 사실에도 불구하고 (비록 ~지만)
Even though I had money, I didn't spend $200 on shoes.
Even if #확인되지 않은 사실을 놓고 가정 (만약에 ~라고 해도)
Even if I had money, I wouldn't spend $200 on shoes.
Even if I fix it, it won't work well.
Whether Whether you are rich or poor, you deserve to be happy.
